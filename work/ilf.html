Indigenous Literacy Foundation

How might we help potential partners in navigating the website, so they feel confident that they have all the information they need to become partners?

 
 

Overview

The Indigenous Literacy Foundation (ILF) is an Australian not-for-profit organisation that works to address the educational disadvantages faced by Indigenous Australian children and young people by providing access to books and literacy programs.

Challenge

ILF is struggling to increase its corporate partnership numbers.

Solution

Uncover partner’s pain points, explore possible solutions, and develop a responsive website that allows users to become partners without calling the ILF office for basic information.

Outcome

Increased partnership engagement and enhanced user confidence by redesigning pages and improving navigation.

Design Process

Before starting the design process we contacted ILF to gain some insight into any issues they may already be having with the website.

They mentioned that they would like to improve the partnership process and they are currently updating their website.

This confirmed that the partner process does need a redesign.

1-on-1 Qualitative Interviews and Usability Testing

We interviewed five users and afterward asked them to complete the task of becoming a partner on the ILF website for usability testing.

Users failed to complete the task of becoming a partner on the ILF website.

“When I clicked onto 'how you can help' and then found 'partner with us' it was unclear as to how to become partner, the contact us for more information felt like I wasn't actually becoming a partner".

“If I'm trying to partner it shouldn't take me back to individual donations. I am so confused I just thought there would be a clear way to partner"

“It is misleading. They want us to call or email the office if we are interested in becoming a partner, then why provide a partnership option on the website?”

Competitive Analysis

We reviewed existing solutions and design patterns applied by similar websites to identify opportunities and solutions

Key take away

  • Clearly communicate organisation values.
  • Explain the impact existing partners are making.
  • Encourage partners to engage and take action.
  • Show where their dollars are going.

Conducted SWOT analysis to gain insight into main competitors.

Feature Prioritisation

This happened quite organically as we took all of our research and prioritised the features based on value and effort.

New Sitemap

From our research, it was clear that we needed to make the partnership path a lot simpler and provide quick access to the partnership information.

We referenced results from the card sorting to identify the hierarchy and build a new sitemap.

Partnership user path

New Design Insight

  • Allow users to start the process of becoming a partner today.
  • Allow users to have clear options when becoming a partner.
  • Put ‘partners' in an intuitive place so users do not have to go searching.
  • Provide clear information about partnerships.

Low Fidelity Mockups

Based on the new sitemap, mockups were designed to see how partnership pages within the website will flow.

Key Test Results and Iterations

We created interactive low-fidelity wireframes to conduct usability testing with five users. The goal of the testing was to help users feel more encouraged to become a partner.

  • Users said that “Ways To Donate” indicates the monetary donations section and can create confusion. The category name for partnership pages should be intuitive and clear.
  • Users wanted real-life stories on the home page to feel a connection to the cause.
